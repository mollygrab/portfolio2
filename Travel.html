Study abroad and other travels

In the fall of my junior year, I studied abroad in Galway, Ireland. Living and attending college in Galway was a once-in-a-lifetime experience that helped to shape my entire college career.

Galway

Galway is the third largest city in Ireland after Dublin and Cork. However, while Dublin and Cork feel much more urban, Galway is more like a big town. The National University of Ireland, Galway, which I attended for a semester, draws students from all over Ireland as well as the rest of the world, and these college-age students make up a large part of Galway's population.

Galway Bay

Galway is located on the West Coast of Ireland, which offered me a unique perspective on Irish language and history. In one of my classes, I was able to study the ebb and flow of the Irish language throughout the country and examine the political contexts in which it flourished, and those in which it almost died out. This subject was fascinating, and the stunning landscape of the West of Ireland was an added bonus.

The National University of Ireland, Galway

I took a full course load of five classes at NUIG in the fall of 2015. My classes included Romanticism, bioethics, British Literature, moral and political philosophy, and historical Irish culture. NUIG is one of the only fully bilingual universities (offering classes in both English and Irish/Gaelic) in Ireland.

The Best Places in Galway

Before leaving Galway, I made sure to rank some of my favorite spots.

  • 5. Penney's - This clothing store was trendy and affordable, and even if some things I bought there fell apart in our apartment's tiny washer, it was worth it.
  • 4. The Front Door Pub - I didn't know that beef and Guinness stew existed before I went to Ireland, and now I question how I live without The Front Door's.
  • 3. Java's - Another thing I didn't know before I came to Ireland: that I would need to plan for weekly crepes from Java's in my budget.
  • 2. McDonagh's Seafood - I don't like seafood, but McDonagh's chicken and chips changed my life.
  • 1. Charlie Byrne's Bookstore - Voted the best bookstore in Ireland for a reason; my suitcase was lined with books on my way back to America.
